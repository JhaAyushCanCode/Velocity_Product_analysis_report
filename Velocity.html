Executive Summary

Project Velocity is a comprehensive product lifecycle simulation designed to solve the critical business problem of high churn among "Mid-Tier" users in the Indian quick-commerce market.

Target Market
Indian Quick-Commerce
Expected Impact
5% Churn Reduction

Executive Summary

The Indian quick-commerce market, projected to grow from USD 136.43 billion in 2025 to USD 327.38 billion by 2030 434, faces a critical retention challenge. Mid-Tier users—those who have made 2-4 purchases—are churning at an alarming rate of 25% per month, primarily due to "shipping cost shock" at checkout.

Key Insight

Existing loyalty programs rely on traditional points-based systems that are slow to deliver value and fail to address the immediate psychological barriers to purchase completion.

The "Velocity Loyalty Program" addresses this gap through predictive behavioral analytics and gamified instant rewards. By analyzing user behavior patterns and delivering personalized, immediate incentives, we can reduce churn by 5% and increase customer lifetime value by 10%.

5. Product Requirement Document: The "Blueprint"

5.1 Problem Statement & Objective

Problem Statement

High churn rate of 25% per month among Mid-Tier users after their 3rd purchase, primarily driven by "shipping cost shock" at checkout.

Impact: Significant loss of potential revenue from users who have already shown purchase intent.

Objective

Design and launch the "Velocity Loyalty Program" to reduce churn by 5% and increase lifetime value by 10% for Mid-Tier users within one year.

Success Criteria: Measurable improvement in retention rates and customer lifetime value.

5.2 User Stories

Epic: Rewarding Loyalty Experience

"As a Mid-Tier user, I want to feel rewarded for my loyalty so that I continue to shop on the app"

This epic encapsulates the core user need that the Velocity Loyalty Program addresses, focusing on creating value and appreciation for users in their critical customer journey phase.

Story 1: Progress Visualization

"As a user, I want to see my progress toward a reward so that I am motivated to add more items"

AC1: Progress bar displayed on cart page
AC2: Shows remaining amount for free shipping
AC3: Updates in real-time with cart changes

Story 2: Instant Rewards

"As a user, I want to receive instant, behavior-based rewards so that I feel valued"

AC1: System triggers rewards automatically
AC2: In-app and email notifications
AC3: Rewards auto-apply to next purchase

5.3 Functional Requirements

FR1: Real-time Tracking

System must capture and process user events (page views, add to cart, purchase) in real-time to update progress and trigger rewards.

Technical Note: Event-driven architecture with <200ms processing latency

FR2: Progress Updates

Progress bar on cart page must update within <200ms when user modifies cart contents.

Performance Requirement: <200ms latency for seamless user experience

FR3: Rules Engine

System must evaluate user behavior against predefined conditions and automatically trigger rewards when criteria are met.

Example Rule: IF 3 purchases AND last purchase < 30 days THEN grant 10% discount
